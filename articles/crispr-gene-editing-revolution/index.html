CRISPR stands for Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats - a mouthful that describes nature's own immune system for bacteria. Scientists have repurposed this bacterial defense mechanism into the most precise gene-editing tool ever created.

Types of Gene Editing ğŸ¯

Base Editing ğŸ“

Single letter changes without cutting DNA:

  • Cytosine base editors - C to T conversions
  • Adenine base editors - A to G conversions
  • Accounts for 60% of disease-causing mutations
  • More precise than traditional CRISPR

Prime Editing âœï¸

The "word processor" of gene editing:

  • Insert, delete, or replace up to 300 DNA letters
  • Extremely precise with fewer off-target effects
  • Can fix 89% of known disease-causing mutations
  • Developed by David Liu's lab in 2019

Epigenetic Editing ğŸ¨

Changing gene expression without altering DNA sequence:

  • dCas9 - "Dead" Cas9 that doesn't cut
  • Activators or repressors attached to turn genes on/off
  • Reversible modifications that can be undone
  • Useful for studying gene function

Large-scale genetic changes:

  • Deletions - Removing large DNA segments
  • Inversions - Flipping chromosomal sections
